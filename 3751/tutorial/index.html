Introduction to Blender

This screencast video introduces basic operations with the 3d modelling and animation program Blender, including:

  • Manipulating objects in the scene
  • Adding and Deleting objects from the scene
  • Joining, duplicating, and scaling objects
  • Applying the smooth operation to objects
  • Rendering an image, and the relationship between the camera, scene, and light sources

Pedagogical Concerns

This material relates to a number of specific curriculum outcomes from the Grade 7 Communications Technology Module.

  • 1.07 - demonstrate understanding of how communications technologies are used to create, modify and disseminate information
  • 1.12 - demonstrate understanding of the purpose of technical drawings
  • 2.01 - use a range of 2-dimensional and 3-dimensional representational techniques to communicate technical solutions and ideas

Blender is is primarily used as an animation tool, but it is suitable for creating 3d mockups and design models. A 3d model can be the best tool to communicate the design and proportion of a complicated object.
